As Kim Kardashian announces she's pregnant again, a look back at her maternity style

 Posted on September 17, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  1057 words  |  Aldo Pusey

After months of attempts, Kim Kardashian has revealed that she is pregnant with her second child with husband Kanye West.

The 34-year-old reality star dropped the bombshell news right at the end of Sunday's mid-season finale of Keeping Up With The Kardashians on E!.

As well as morning sickness and juggling her pregnancy with her duties as a working mother-of-one, Kim will have to contend with maternity style - something even she admitted she struggled with the first time around.

When she was carrying her first child, North West, in 2013, Kim took heat from the tabloids and armchair fashion critics for her maternity look. 

The mother-of-one's maternity style was seemingly a continuation of her usual parade of body-hugging dresses - many of which have a hard-to-wear hemline that hits below the knee - tucked-in blouses, complicated couture-like details and super-high heels.

How could we forget her curtain-inspired Givenchy Met Ball gown? 

Kim's outfit was slated by fashion critics, with the late funny man Robin Williams weighing in saying that he wore the look better as cross-dressing octogenarian nanny Euphegenia Doubtfire.

The stiletto-loving star came under fire by the fashion police once more in May 2013 when she tottered out in a pair of sky-high perspex high heels

Tight dresses, such as the grey one she wore at the DuJour Magazine Spring 2013 Issue Celebration at the Darby in New York in March 2013, left, did her little favours, whilst a black and white shift dress didn't go down well with fashion fans, right 

Kim's peplum trousers were far from flattering when teamed with this tight black polo neck

And then, Vogue took the critique one step further by cropping the star out of their best dressed gallery. 

Vogue.com collated a glamorous slideshow of the event’s 77 best dressed guests which, unsurprisingly, did not include the reality TV star.

It did, however, include Kim's then boyfriend Kanye West who, like Kim, was decked head-to-toe in Givenchy.

The style bible hailed Kanye for his exemplary sartorial prowess - but very deliberately cropped his pregnant beau from the shot - and did not include her elsewhere.

Kim Kardashian looked super stylish in leather trousers and a feathered top at the Topshop/Topman LA Opening Party At Cecconi's in Los Angeles in February 2013

Kim flatters her form in a sleek black dress, left, out and about in New York, and looks casually chic in white jeans and a cream top in Los Angeles in April 2013, right

The only evidence that Kim was there on the night is a flash of floral and a disembodied arm linking with Kanye's to the left of the frame. 

Vogue may not bave been impressed by her floral faux pas - but the designer behind the top-to-toe look, Givenchy's Riccardo Tisci, defended the look to WWD, saying, 'I think she looked amazing.

'She was the most beautiful pregnant woman I dressed in my career.'

The stiletto-loving star came under fire by the fashion police once more in May 2013 when she tottered out in a pair of sky-high perspex high heels, and while she showed little pain in her face, her feet did all of the talking.

The star's feet were so swollen that she barely managed to squeeze them into the footwear.

Puffy, her ankles were also clearly suffering, even appearing severely pinched in the stylish shoes. 

Kim failed to impress in a silk black coat dress at the E! Upfront event in April 2013, left, but got in right showcasing her off-duty style at the Dorchester hotel in London in May 2013, right 

Kim quickly figure out that black was the most flattering colour for her during her first pregnancy

Despite her naysayers, there were times when the usually stylish star got it very right. 

When she wasn't suffering the odd wardrobe malfunction, Kim was committed to a uniform of sleek suiting and stilettos. 

The star, who is a huge fan of designer dressing, often flattered her frame in draped tops and off-duty T-shirts with slick trousers by the likes of Saint Laurent. 

Her choice of sculptural coats, too, were a chic way to conceal her growing baby bump and she quickly realised that all black was the secret formula for maternity dress success. 

Here's to another stylish nine months.  

THE ULTIMATE GUIDE TO MATERNITY STYLE 

Pea in the Pod design director Olivia Capone Myers says stretchy leggings are the No. 1 must-have item for a successful pre- and post-baby wardrobe.

Leggings, along with side-ruched T-shirts and dresses, preferably made of stretch jersey, are the first things to buy when the bump starts showing, and they're the last ones you're wearing after mom and baby are settled in at home, Myers says.

The key, experts say, is to stay true to your personal style, but not be so stubborn that you won't tweak it.

Kimono sleeves instantly create the bohemian vibe that works well in maternity wear, and so do tunic tops with a blousoned dropped waist. The high necks and below-the-knee hemlines Kardashian sometimes wears do not.

Dangling earrings would draw the eye to the slope of the neck and shoulders, and a chunky necklace worn against a jewel neck or slightly scooped or V-neck shirt would emphasize the bustline upward.

Fit is also important. Stay away from extremes - whether it's too tight or too loose, says designer Rebecca Minkoff, who blogs for Baby Center.

Oversized clothes might fit the bump, but will work against everywhere else, agrees de Swaan Arons. Consider this the time to show off nice legs or a bit of cleavage.

Jersey fabric with its tight-weave stretch is good camouflage (and not just for pregnant women). Designer Abi Ferrin says good jersey will stretch and shrink with the body, and that the weight of the fabric smooths bumps.

The garment should be cut to give a little bit of structure because the jersey is so forgiving, Ferrin says.
