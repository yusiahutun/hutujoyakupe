    How to Choose the Best Nail Polish for Stamping

    Choosing the best nail polish for stamping can make a significant difference in the success of your nail art. When selecting a nail polish you may look for the following features:

  • Type of formulation: Look for nail polishes that offer intense pigmentation, quick-drying, and long-lasting formula that easily transfers onto nails. These polishes will provide better coverage and ensure that the stamped design comes out vividly and clearly on your nails. They will also enhance stamping precision and speed, and prevent smudging or smearing during the stamping process as well.
  • Ingredients: Look for nail polishes labeled as "5-free" or "10-free," which means they are free from harmful chemicals like formaldehyde, toluene, DBP, camphor, and formaldehyde resin. Non-toxic and water-based nail polishes are also a good option for those who prefer more natural and eco-friendly products.

    • Texture: For stamping, it's best to avoid nail polishes with a very thick or gloopy texture, as they may not spread evenly on the stamping plate and could lead to a messy design. Choose a nail polish that has a smooth and consistent texture. It should be easy to apply on the stamping plate and transfer to your nails seamlessly.

    How to Use Regular Nail Polish for Stamping

    To use regular polish for nail stamping you may follow the following steps: 

    1. Begin with applying a specialized base coat, and nail polish gel. Then cure it with a UV lamp.

    2. Apply a layer of latex peel-off tape on the nail edge to prevent staining your skin.

    3. Use the regular polish gel and apply it on the stamping plate.

    4. Scrape off the excess nail polish using the scraper.

    5. After that, take a stamper and press it against the stamping plate to pick up the pattern.

    6. Now use the stamper to press on your nails and transfer it on them. Cure this under the UV lamp.

    7. After it has dried, carefully peel off the nail latex.

    8. Lastly, apply a top coat and cure it under the UV lamp to finish the stamping nail art

    FAQ's

    Do I need special nail polish for stamping?

    Yes, special nail polish formulated for stamping is recommended as it often has the right consistency and pigmentation for clear and crisp designs.

    Can I use gel nail polish for stamping?

    Gel nail polish can be used for stamping, but it may not work as effectively as regular stamping polish due to differences in formulation.

    Is nail stamping Polish different than regular polish?

    Nail stamping polish is often designed to have higher pigmentation and a thicker consistency than regular polish to enhance the transfer of images onto the nails.

    How do you thicken nail polish for stamping?

    To thicken nail polish for stamping, you can add a small amount of clear nail polish or purchase a nail polish thickener to achieve the desired consistency.

    How long does stamping polish take to dry?

    Stamping polish typically dries quickly, usually within a few minutes, but the exact drying time can vary depending on the brand and formulation.

    How do you keep your nail polish from chipping?

    To prevent nail polish from chipping, apply a base coat, ensure each layer is fully dry before applying the next, and finish with a top coat. Additionally, avoid exposing your nails to activities that may cause chipping, such as excessive water exposure or rough handling.
