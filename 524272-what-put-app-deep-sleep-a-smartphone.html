What does it mean to put an app in deep sleep on a smartphone?

 Posted on July 18, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  700 words  |  Martina Birk

If you constantly switch between apps all day, your device's battery will drain quickly. Fortunately, you can set your apps to deep sleep so they don't run in the background and drain your battery life. But what does it mean to put an app in deep sleep on a smartphone?

One of the best ways to conserve battery consumption and reduce data usage in a mobile phone is by putting apps to deep sleep. The technique helps prevent an app from running in the phone's background, preventing it from sending notifications.

What does it mean to put an app in deep sleep?

To put an app in deep sleep means to stop or prevent the app installed in a mobile phone from running in the phone's background until the user opens the app. This means the app will not receive updates through the Play Store or send notifications or messages on the phone. This ensures that your phone lasts longer.

What happens if you put an app in deep sleep?

When an app is put in deep sleep, it will not run in your mobile phone background or be active as it has been put to a stop, though not disabled. Consequently, it will delay background updates and notifications until you wake the apps again.

Advantages of putting apps to deep sleep

Should you put apps into deep sleep? Yes, deep sleep mode offers several benefits. Below are some benefits of putting your apps into deep sleep.

  • Improved device performance. It enhances the overall performance of your phone by reducing resource consumption and improving app responsiveness. It also improves device performance by minimizing background processing.
  • Reduced battery consumption: It helps extend battery life by reducing your phone's workload since it does not have to distribute resources to apps that don't need them.
  • Minimizes data usage: Putting an app in a deep sleep reduces background data usage as it reduces background app activity.
  • Efficient resource allocation: By efficiently allocating resources, an app can upgrade its overall capacity and responsiveness. Additionally, it allows an app to diminish unneeded use of system resources.

How to put an app in deep sleep on Android

If your Android phone seems to be slowing down or unresponsive, it could be because of many apps working in the background. You can fix the problem by putting some apps to deep sleep. Here are a few steps on how to do it using a Samsung phone.

  • Open the settings app on your Home Screen to access the app you want to optimize.
  • Scroll down to device care. For other Androids, you should scroll down to the battery.
  • Tap on the device care and select battery by tapping on it.
  • Next, tap on app power management, and right there, you will see put unused apps to sleep.
  • Select put unused apps to sleep on your Android phone.
