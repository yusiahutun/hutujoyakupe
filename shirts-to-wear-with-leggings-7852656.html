22 Shirts to Wear with Leggings for Any Occasion

 Posted on August 4, 2024  |  7 minutes  |  1370 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Life is hard and, sometimes, real pants are simply out of the question. For the days when buttons, zippers, and denim are just too much to handle, it’s helpful to have a few leggings-centric looks in your outfit arsenal. While we’ve already established that soft pants, leggings included, work for most occasions, including days you head into the office, it isn’t always easy to determine what shirts go with leggings. The good news is that there’s a vibe-dependent component to putting together a leggings look. The less good news is that in a world with fashion vibes and aesthetics aplenty, it can be tricky to know where to start.

There are three styles of shirts that work best when paired with leggings:

  • Cropped tees, hoodies, and sweaters are a natural fit in a leggings outfit, especially if your leggings are high-waisted.
  • Oversized shirts also play nicely with leggings, creating a silhouette that'll make you look leggy, no matter how tall you are.
  • Fitted tops can be worn with leggings, although they are the least commonly spotted of the three and are a bit tricky to style.

    • Whether you're looking for a shirt that will complete a cozy weekend lounge look or a top that can transform a leggings outfit into an elevated ensemble, we've got you covered, (crop) top to bottom with the 22 best shirts to wear with leggings, below.

    A Statement Peplum Top

    Since a peplum top creates a lot of volume, they're one of our favorite shirts to wear with a pair of sleek black leggings. When picking a peplum top for this look, we recommend seeking out one with plenty of structure for a contrasting and dramatic silhouette.

    A Cable Knit Sweater

    Cozy girls, this one's for you. An oversized cable-knit sweater is a perfect shirt to wear with leggings when temps start to drop, but it's not actually cold out. When worn with leggings, you can easily dress up a cable-knit sweater with elevated, classic accessories or lean into a more rugged and outdoorsy feel with ankle boots and a beanie.

    A Crewneck Pullover

    Leggings and sneakers go together like peanut butter and jelly and we love the look of a classically sporty crewneck sweatshirt thrown into the mix. Inject a little '90s into your styling by layering a turtleneck top underneath.

    An Embellished Crop Top

    Embellished separates aren't just for parties. You can create a modern, unexpected outfit by pairing a crop top with rhinestone details with a vibrant blazer and leggings. We love the blend of hard and soft created when this look is worn with sturdy boots, but you can create a more feminine feel with a pair of heels.

    A Cropped Cami

    Embrace a bit of boho energy by pairing a crocheted or knit camisole — or two! — with a long coat, boots, and leggings. When it comes to this outfit, the more layers, the merrier, so don't shy away from adding a sweater or jacket to the mix.

    A Tunic-Length Top

    Looking for a way to wear that not-quite-a-sweater-dress sweater or tunic? Pull on a pair of leggings and you're good to go. If you're concerned about a shirt or sweater that hits below your hips making you look boxy, a long, fitted jacket can add a more refined silhouette.

    A Turtleneck Crop Top

    Crop tops tend to read as casual, but a fresh and unexpected neckline can switch things up. A long-sleeved crop top with a structured or oversized turtleneck delivers fashion girlie energy to a leggings outfit without sacrificing a snuggle-forward feel.

    A Collegiate Cardigan

    This varsity jacket-adjacent top sits halfway between athleisure and preppy style, especially when worn with sneakers and leggings. While it's definitely casual, a cardigan, especially with a vintage, collegiate vibe, is a world away from your standard sweatshirt and leggings outfit.

    A Short-Sleeved Knit Top

    A short-sleeved knit top and a pair of pumps can transform a leggings outfit from one you'd wear running errands to a date night-approved look, especially if you accessorize with a coordinating statement bag and elevated jewelry. A lightweight knit shirt can also be tucked in for a snatched silhouette without looking bulky at the waistband.

    A Layered Button-Down

    What's better than one oversized shirt worn with leggings? Obviously, two oversized tops. A loose-fitting button-down and baggy cropped sweater manage to look put-together and intentional, despite not being fitted or formal.

    A Knotted Blouse

    If you tend to get lost in oversized tops, knotting an oversized shirt or blouse at your waist is an easy fix. This styling hack will lengthen the lines of your leggings so you're not left looking like a little kid in a sleep shirt.

    A Sweater Vest

    Another great way to accentuate your curves when wearing an oversized shirt with leggings is to add a cropped sweater vest to the mix. Since it doesn't have sleeves, you'll still get the oversized look of the shirt worn underneath, but with a bit more of a tailored vibe.

    A Three-Quarter Sleeve Sweater

    For a refined leggings look, try pairing your leggings with a fitted sweater that has three-quarter-length sleeves and adding a statement belt. The final effect is very Roman Holiday with all the comfort of your favorite sweatpants.

    A Boxy Blazer

    Take advantage of leggings' close-fit with a slouchy sweater worn with a boxy blazer. This look reads business casual, especially if you happen to have a vintage style doctor bag or briefcase on hand.

    A Matching Hoodie

    If you have a pair of printed or patterned leggings, it's generally a good idea to wear them with a solid top, like a cozy hoodie. Stick to coordinated shoes and accessories to avoid this outfit looking too busy.

    A Cropped Tee

    A cropped T-shirt is an essential shirt to have on hand if you frequently wear leggings. You can dress this look up with a blazer and heels, keep it casual by adding a cardigan, or lean into a sporty vibe without any outerwear layers, but with the addition of crew socks and sneakers.

    A Leather Shirt

    For an undeniably elevated look, you can't go wrong with a leather shirt and matching leggings. Black is an easy color choice here, but this look works equally well in jewel tones.

    When it comes to the fit of the leather shirt in question, you've got options, but a more standard fit, rather than something cropped or oversized, with add balance to this look.

    A Corduroy Button-Down

    A little less heavyweight than a full-on shacket, an oversized corduroy button-down is a great shirt to toss on over a sports bra and leggings. It creates a sporty, casual look that's more dressed up than gym clothes, but just as comfortable.

    A Sleeveless Khaki Top

    For a sharp look with a bit of an utilitarian edge, try wearing a sleeveless khaki shirt with leggings. You'll get more great outdoorsy energy if you add a pair of duck boots, but you can also go for no-nonsense business chic with a pair of spiked high-heels.

    A Soft, Graphic Tee

    There's nothing wrong with keeping a leggings outfit entirely casual and tossing on an ultra-soft and faded graphic tee. That said, this normcore ensemble can achieve a bit of fashion street cred if you choose a T-shirt with a designer logo rather than album art, and add a pair of stacked sneakers.

    A Striped Shirt or Sweater

    There's something about a striped sweater, especially in shades of red or blue and white, that just feels elevated. Whether you opt for a sweater or a long-sleeve striped T-shirt, you'll look put-together and, with a blazer, shades, and trendy loafers, even quietly luxurious.

    A Color-Blocked Sweatshirt

    If you add a color-blocked shirt or sweatshirt and a color-blocked blazer to a leggings outfit, no one will notice you're not wearing a smart pair of trousers. With a coordinating crossbody bag and block heels, this is one leggings outfit that bears repeating.
