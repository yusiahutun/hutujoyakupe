Viral Photo of Over 25 Helicopters Was Not Captured at Prestigious St Andrews School, Turi

 Posted on January 31, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  421 words  |  Aldo Pusey
  • A Twitter user posted a photo showing over 25 helicopters and luxury vehicles on a field while claiming it was captured in Kenya
  • The Tweep claimed that the photo was from a visiting day at the Prestigious St Andrews School, Turi, in Molo, Nakuru county
  • However, a reverse image search by TUKO.co.ke established that the information was misleading and the location where it was captured

A viral photo doing rounds in Kenya’s social media space depicts over 25 helicopters and luxury cars parked on a field.

Parents land in choppers during Turi visiting day?

Several Caucasian people could be seen milling around the vehicles and choppers on the well-manicured grass.

A black building, seemingly a hangar, could be seen in one corner of the photo.

A Twitter user who posted the image claimed it was captured at St Andrew School, Turi.

“Imagine this is just a normal Visiting day at St Andrews Turi in Kenya,” wrote @omwambaKE.

Truth behind viral chopper photo

However, a reverse image search by TUKO.co.ke established that the image was captured in Europe.

It was posted on LinkedIn by MD Helicopters, an aviation and aerospace component manufacturing company.

“We are a leading manufacturer of commercial, military, law enforcement and air-rescue helicopters,” their bio reads.

The company hosted the event bringing together its clients.
