Who was Bumpy Johnson?

Ellsworth Raymond Johnson (31 October 1905 to 7 July 1968) was born to William Johnson and Margaret Moultrie in Charleston, South Carolina.

Ellsworth, famous as Bumpy Johnson, was an American organized crime boss in Harlem, New York. He came to power during the 1930s and was known for his brutality. Bumpy Johnson was among the most powerful criminals of his time.

He earned the moniker "Godfather of Harlem" because of his reign/ domination in the NYC neighbourhood, Harlem. In 2019, a TV series titled Godfather of Harlem was created following his story.

Bumpy Johnson's wife

Bumpy Johnson's wife, Mayme Hatcher Johnson, was a native of North Carolina but spent most of her life in Harlem. Mrs Johnson was born in 1914 in NC and relocated to New York City in 1938, where she worked as a waitress in a club owned by actress/ singer Ethel Waters.

In 1948, she met and married Bumpy Johnson, the legendary gangster depicted in TV movies American Gangster, Hoodlum and The Cotton Club.

In 2004, Mayme Johnson relocated to Philadelphia, and three years later, she decided to write a biography about Bumpy Johnson so that people would know the real him rather than all the myths.
