You probably think of the months from September through January as fall and winter, and obviously, you’re not wrong. I, however, prefer to think of the chilly half of the year as denim season. Forget pumpkin spice lattes (I’m kidding, I would never), the second the temperature starts to drop below 80 degrees, I’m pulling my jeans out. The right pair of jeans has the ability to transform my look and my confidence—that’s why I’m the first to get on board with the denim trends for fall and winter of 2023. I’ll be sipping my pumpkin spice latte in style. 

I’m relieved to report that baggy denim is still the denim to be wearing in the fall and winter of 2023. Sorry skinny jeans, you’re not back in style just yet (though I’m sure the time will eventually come). While you can still wear a simple pair of baggy jeans, the trend has taken on new formats for a fresh fall look. Carpenter jeans and balloon pants are two of the newest trendy silhouettes to join the baggy jeans family. Both styles are the epitome of cool comfort, so there’s really no excuse not to give them a try. 

If you’re already a street style expert, you’ll know that denim skirts have already been a major trend for 2023. You can expect to see the polarizing style continue to dominate your Instagram feed this fall and winter but with a twist. A-line silhouettes and midi hemlines are joining the denim skirt trend group chat. 

Another controversial trend to make the shortlist—or should I say “jort list”—are jorts. A jort can be defined as a pair of long denim shorts and whether you like it or not, the cool kids are wearing them. If you need outfit inspiration, I recommend searching for pictures of Hailey Bieber and Bella Hadid in the short style. 

And finally, you can’t have a successful fall and winter denim season without a rendezvous with a Canadian tuxedo. Pair your favorite denim jacket trend with your denim skirt or jeans for a not-so-formal ‘fit.

  • Carpenter Jeans

    You want your outfit to work right? And by work, I mean werk. Whether you’re headed out for a day of manual home improvements (unlikely) or want to borrow from the workwear aesthetic (probable), carpenter jeans are the cool pants to wear this fall. The style is meant to look lived in—think baggy fit, tattered hemlines, and plenty of functional pockets. Carpenter pants are the perfect style for the big pant-tiny top outfit formula. Throw on a simple black tank, a pair of black kitten heels or sneakers, and a statement bag, and call it a look! All in a day’s work.

  • Balloon Jeans

    Balloon pants are the denim equivalent of parachute pants and let me just say, they are incredible. The purposefully balloon-like silhouette of fabric that curves out from the hip and tapers back in at the ankle is chic, comfortable, and the easiest way to elevate even the simplest of outfits. A balloon jean is like a baggy jean but with purpose. You may think the style looks a bit silly now but I guarantee you by the time November rolls around, you’ll be buying a pair.

  • Jorts

    Yes, you read that right: jorts are fashionable. Just a few years ago, the style was exclusively worn by Adam Sandler, now you’ll see jorts outside of every trendy New York City restaurant and in street-style photos of celebrities like Hailey Bieber. The key to wearing jorts is balance. Pair them with a frilly top or heels to off-set the inherently grungy energy jorts exude.
