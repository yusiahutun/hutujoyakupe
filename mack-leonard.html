Throughout her career, she consistently displayed her ability to contribute to the team’s success, recording multiple doubles and home runs in key games and driving crucial runs with her batting prowess.

Early Life

Mack Leonard was born on September 27, 1999, in Bloomington, Illinois, as the daughter of the late Kenneth Leonard, Jr. and Lana Leonard. She has a sister named Machayla, who played volleyball for Illinois State University. Mack’s family has a strong sports background, with her father competing in track and field at Illinois State from 1989 to 1993, winning the MVC Decathlon Championship in 1991 and finishing as the runner-up in 1992 and 1993. Her mother, Lana, also played softball for Illinois State from 1989 to 1993 under the guidance of Coach Fischer.

Mack Leonard Career

In her career as a softball player, Mack was consistently recognized for her exceptional skills since her high school days. Her contributions on the field were recognized when she was named the WJBC High School Athlete of the Week on April 18, 2016. She earned a 1.13 ERA as a sophomore and batted.506 and slugged.899 with eight home runs and 48 RBIs as a junior. She appeared in 26 games for the Redbirds as a true freshman, making 18 starts in the circle. Mack Leonard received the MVC Newcomer of the Week award on March 11 in recognition of her performance in the FAU/FIU Parents’ Weekend Tournament.

She received three starts in the circle, pitched the first complete game of her career against Florida Atlantic, and struck out a season-high three hitters on four occasions. Mack participated in all 41 of the Redbirds’ contests and was selected to the All-MVC First Team. Her single-season batting average is third-best at ISU all-time, and she placed in the top five in the MVC in every significant statistical category. She had a team-high 18 multi-hit games, 10 multi-RBI games, and finished the season with a 26-game string of reaching base.

Rise to Fame

She had the 27th-best national on-base percentage (.532) and the 34th-best batting average. In the circle, she went 11-4 with a 3.33 ERA.  She struck out 80 hitters in 100.0 innings of work in 21 appearances and 17 starts, pitching nine full games, six shutouts, and one save. Leonard started the game against Florida with an RBI single to right, and then she blasted the first ball she saw for her fifth home run of the year, a three-run shot.
