What happened to Jana Reynolds?

May 5, 1988, was an ordinary day for Jana Reynolds and her husband, Jeff. Jeff left the house for work around 10:00 pm and bade her wife goodbye with a kiss as he usually did. However, upon arrival at his house the following morning, he noticed something strange.

His wife's car was still in the carport, which was not ordinary. But, by the time Jeff comes home from work, his wife is already gone to school. So, he advanced to his house, wondering what might have caused his wife not to go to school.

The first reg flag as he was entering the house was the back door. He realized that it was broken into. And he was met with the terrible sight after making his way to the bedroom. Jana was lying on the bed in a pool of blood.

It is ordinary for murder cases to have the husband as one of the prime suspects. Bu not Jana Reynold's case. Jeff had a profound alibi as he was at work the entire time. Therefore, investigators eliminated him from the list of possible suspects and started working on other people.

According to the police medical examiner, Jana Reynolds was murdered between 12:00 am and 3:00 pm. Also, it was hard determining the motive for the murder as the family was quiet and minded their business.

As police started interviewing people, they zeroed in on Albert McDaniels, who was seen in the area the night of the murder. He voluntarily gave the police his statement and provided blood, pubic hair, and saliva samples.

The police ran tests on the samples and returned negative results. So Albert was put aside as the man behind the killing.

Police then tested samples from another suspect, Joe Tucker, who was seen in the area that night. He gave his hair sample, which was tested and failed to match the fragment discovered at the scene. However, despite his sample not being a match, the pathologist insisted that he couldn't be ruled out without pubic hair samples.

Who murdered Jana Reynolds?

Unfortunately, Jana Reynolds's murder case went cold for 13 years after her murder. The Mount Vernon Police Department decided to revive the case in 2001 as, this time, an alternative light source was available.

The investigators reviewed Jana's pyjama pants and underwear and discovered stains that they confirmed to be seminal fluid. It was enough to provide better DNA evidence for the case than what they could get 13 years ago.

And since they had not ruled out Albert, they tested his samples again against the new evidence. Once again, it did not match, and they eliminated him as a suspect.

They compared it to the DNA of Joe Tucker and found a match, and they had their killer. Investigators tracked Joe Tucker in 2002 and arrested him for the murder of Jana Reynolds.

Joe Tucker’s trial

Joe Tucker did not admit guilt but stated that he knew Jana as they had worked together before. While Joe Tucker was waiting to go to trial for the murder of Jana, he was sent to prison for parole violation. While incarcerated, he opened up to another inmate detailing everything about the case. And the detainee shared the document with the prosecutors.
