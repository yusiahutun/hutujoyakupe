Alessandra Colon On TLC’s Crack Addicts

TLC’s Crack Addicts isn’t what it sounds like. The show follows chiropractor Alessandra Colon as she and her staff approach some of the most extreme and debilitating issues with one-of-a-kind, groundbreaking methods.

“Dr. Colon provides unparalleled pain relief, improves the quality of life, and sends patients home happier and healthier,” TLC summarized the show. “Get ready for a jaw-dropping reality show, coming to TLC, where bones crack, joints pop, and bodies twist into great positions. With a touch of humor, Dr. Colon heals patients using the power of chiropractic adjustment.”

In the premiere ep, Alessandra had an appointment with Brenda, and as she was clicking her back, the patient farted straight into her face! But like a true professional, she took the incident in light spirits and said, “I was not expecting her to fart in my face, but I was gonna take one for the team.”

Obviously, the patient, who was giggling frantically, apologized for letting the air out.

How Much Is Alessandra Colon Net Worth?

Alessandra Colon garnered a net worth of under $1 million by 2023.

She’s been working as a chiropractor at Papa Chiropractic ever since October 2016. There, she confronts some of the weirdest and most extraordinary cases and tries her best to bring pain relief and life-altering transformations to the lives of her patients.

“My mission is to heal people one spine at a time, and have a lot of fun doing it,” she said.

Reportedly, Alessandra made around $153 thousand per annum as a chiropractor in Florida. That’s definitely on the high end of the pay scale. But her journey here wasn’t easy. The chiropractor has faced her own share of health issues and is a cancer survivor.

Alessandra was diagnosed with Stage 4 Hodgkin’s Lymphoma cancer when she was 14 — with only a 14% chance to survive. “The worst part most would think was the chemo and radiation, but I never looked at it that way. Sure, it’s no fun to be near death every day. I mostly just worried about my family,” she said.

But she never lost hope. She completed high school, graduated from the University of Arizona in 2004, and earned her Doctor of Chiropractic from Palmer Chiropractic of Florida in 2012. Now, she’s a reputed chiropractor.

“I made a choice right then and there to either let the bad things in my life take hold or to overcome them,” she said. “It was the first time I knew fear was a matter of perspective and that my future moving forward was a choice. This paved way for the doctor I am today.”

The love they share is unconditional. “I love him just the way he is. He’s always there to support my dreams, make dinner if I can’t, or help me with my constant ever-increasing list,” Alessandra said.
