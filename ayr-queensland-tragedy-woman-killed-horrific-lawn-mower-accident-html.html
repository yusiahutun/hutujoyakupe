Ayr, Queensland: Heartbreak after mother-of-two Kylie Walker is killed in freak lawn mower accident

 Posted on March 3, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  374 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

EXCLUSIVE 

A woman who was killed following a horrific lawn mower accident has been identified as a mother-of-two and local farmer. 

Kylie Walker, 48, suffered critical head and chest injuries on a private property at Ayr, south of Townsville in Queensland, at 6pm on Monday.

Emergency services rushed to the scene but Ms Walker could not be saved.

Daily Mail Australia can reveal Ms Walker, who leaves behind her husband Darren and their two teenage daughters, grew up locally and hails from a family of farmers. 

Photos posted on social media show the family-of-four enjoy holidays together, going on fishing trips, and working on their farm.

Ms Walker appeared to love working in agriculture and frequently shared photos of her posing alongside cows on their property.

Kylie Walker, 48, is pictured with her husband and two children 

Ms Walker is pictured with her husband Darren, whose father was involved in a helicopter crash more than a decade ago

In August, she shared a photo of her alongside a herd of cattle with the caption: 'Do what you love but call it work'.  

In another post, she shared a picture of an artwork reading:' This farm is more than land and crops. It is a family's heritage and future.'

Ms Walker's death comes after tragedy almost struck the family more than a decade ago when her father-in-law, Neville Walker, a local businessman, was in a helicopter crash in 2011.

Mr Walker, an experienced pilot, and his female passenger were on a joy flight when the aircraft encountered trouble and plunged into a cane field in Ayr.

Mr Walker suffered critical injuries, and his passenger serious injuries, with the pair rushed to hospital in stable conditions. 

While both miraculously survived, Darren Walker told media at the time that his father was in a 'touch and go' state after the accident.  

Ms Walker's death marks the second death involving a lawn mower in the state within a week.

A man was killed after becoming trapped under a lawn mower at Tallegalla at Ipswich, south-east Queensland, last Monday.
