The government has announced that Kenyans will start contributing to the Social Health Insurance Fund (SHIF) from July 1, 2024.

Why SHIF contributions were pushed forward

Health Cabinet Secretary (CS) Susan Nakhumicha said on Monday, February 26, that the payments were pushed from March to July to pave the way for talks with the Council of Governors (CoG).

The CS spoke when she spearheaded the national validation exercise on the Social Health Insurance Act draft regulations at the Kenyatta International Convention Centre (KICC), Nairobi.

"Public participation is a crucial aspect of good governance and the validation exercise held today at KICC allowed health stakeholders to review the draft regulations to ensure they reflect the views of all Kenyans.The feedback from participants will form the basis of the Social Health Insurance Regulations 2024 which provides for the implementation of the Primary Healthcare fund, Social Health Insurance Fund emergency, chronic and critical illness Fund," Nakhumicha stated.

Why Kenyans will register afresh

Kenyans must present proof of Social Health Insurance Fund membership to get government services.

The Social Health Insurance Act 2023 provides for mandatory membership of all Kenyans and foreigners residing in the country.

Section 26 (5) of the Act directs the national and county governments to deny services to anyone not registered for SHIF.

The government targets to register 17 million Kenyans above 18 in the new health insurance scheme.

What you need to know about SHIF

  • President William Ruto's administration repealed the National Hospital Insurance Fund (NHIF) to include three funds mandatory for everyone above 18 years.
  • Employees in the formal sector, both public and private, will part with 2.75% of their gross salary as a contribution to the kitty.
  • At the same time, Kenyans without salaries must contribute 2.75% of their annual household income.
  • Kenyans earning a gross salary of KSh 50,000 will contribute KSh 1,375, up from KSh 1,200, while those making over KSh 100,000 will see their amount increase to KSh 2,750.
