PDC World darts championship 2024 prize money: How much do the winner and runner-up earn?

 Posted on March 22, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  306 words  |  Aldo Pusey

The PDC World Darts Championship 2024 has not only showcased the extraordinary talent of 16-year-old Luke Littler but has also positioned him for a substantial financial windfall.

Littler's remarkable journey, which included defeating icons such as Raymond van Barneveld and former world champion Rob Cross, has led him to the final against pre-tournament favorite Luke Humphries.

While his initial goal was merely winning the first round, Littler has now set his sights on the £500,000 winner's prize.

"I was happy to win one game and now I can go all the way," Littler said.

"It's not easy. You are playing Rob, he is a world champion who won on debut. I've got no words."

PDC World darts championship 2024 prize money: What's the total prize?

The total prize pot for the championship is an impressive £2.5 million, with significant earnings for players advancing to the later stages.

Both Littler and Humphries are assured of £200,000, but the stakes escalate dramatically in the final. A captivating performance in the showpiece match could catapult the winner's earnings to a substantial half a million.

Here's the breakdown of the prize money for the various stages of the tournament:

  • Winner: £500,000
  • Runner-Up: £200,000
  • Semi-Final: £100,000
  • Quarter-Final: £50,000
  • Fourth Round: £35,000
  • Third Round: £25,000
  • Second Round: £15,000
  • First Round: £7,500

Where to watch the PDC World Darts Championship final

The PDC World Darts Championship final between Luke Littler and Luke Humphries is scheduled for January 3, 2024, at 20:00.

Fans can catch the action live on Sky Sports Arena and Main Event. Sky Sports customers can also stream the tournament using the Sky Go app on various devices, while NOW offers a contract-free streaming option through computers, smart TVs, phones, and gaming consoles.

The championship's lucrative prize money underscores the significance of this event in the world of professional darts, making it an exciting spectacle for fans and players alike.
