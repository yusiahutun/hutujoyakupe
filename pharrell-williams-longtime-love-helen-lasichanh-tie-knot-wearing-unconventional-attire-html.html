And the bride wore... plaid.

Pharrell Williams tied the knot with longtime love Helen Lasichanh before celebrating in a lavish tartan-filled reception aboard a yacht on Saturday in Miami, Florida.

The beautiful couple stepped away from tradition for the party both wearing different types of the patterned fabric.

Plaid affair: Pharrell Williams and his wife Helen Lasichanh celebrated their wedding aboard a yacht in Florida, wearing different tartans

Helen looked like a princess in a voluminous dark blue and green coat with a white gown peeking through underneath while the 40-year-old singer-songwriter opted for a red and green tartan tuxedo.

Many guests were also snapped in various types of plaid as the boat named 'Never Say Never' launched from Miami.

The couple then allegedly got married later that night at the Kampong National Tropical Botanical Gardens in Coconut Grove.

Several famous friends accompanied the happy couple, according to Us Weekly.

Regal wedding: Helen looked beautiful in a voluminous blue plaid gown

All aboard: Famous friends helped the newlyweds celebrate. In this photo, celeb photographer Terry Richardson, right, is seen getting ready to board the yacht

'Usher and Busta Rhymes did a mini concert at the wedding,' a source said. 'It was so fun. The little concert was amazing. It [was] the most fun wedding I've ever been to.'

Justin Timberlake and Robin Thicke were also rumoured to be performing at the fete.

Pharrell and Helen, who have a four-year-old son named Rocket, have kept their private life largely out of the public eye.

Secret ceremony: Helen and Pharrell reportedly got hitched at a secret ceremony in France earlier this year

The big day: The happy couple wore tartan to say 'I do' in on a luxury yacht which reportedly cost a whopping $19,000 per day to rent

The music mogul has had a busy year. Aside from his nuptials, Pharrell has had a number of collaborations top the charts. He was featured on Daft Punk's song Get Lucky and Robin Thicke's single Blurred Lines as well as two tracks on Jay-z's Magna Carta Holy Grail.

If that wasn't enough, the award-winning artist is also rumoured to be working on a fragrance.

Pharrell recently opened up to Details magazine about his creative process.

Entertaining guests: Party-goers reportedly were treated to performances by Usher and Busta Rhymes

  

Sail away: The 122 ft vessel boasts five state rooms and six bathrooms

'I create based on what I feel like is missing,' he told the mag. 'You jump in and follow your gut. It's like a sculpture—you're just adding on more clay, you're chiseling away and adding on until you feel like it's done, and you stand back and go, "Oh! It's a person."'

The Virginia-born rapper recently dipped his toe into controversy, however, when he defended Miley Cyrus for her twerking ways.

'You have to remember this is a 20-year-old evolving,' he said of Miley during an interview for the documentary Miley: The Movement.' Her dad is Billy Ray Cyrus, her godmother is Dolly Parton, and she’s raised in the era where hip-hop was king.'
