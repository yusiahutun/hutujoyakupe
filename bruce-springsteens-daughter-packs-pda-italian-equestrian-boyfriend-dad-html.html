Bruce Springsteen's daughter packs on the PDA with her Italian equestrian boyfriend IN FRONT of dad

 Posted on August 13, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  641 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Bruce Springsteen and wife Patti spent Easter Weekend in Miami supporting his equestrian daughter and her fellow horse jumping boyfriend as they competed.  

But while Jessica Springsteen, 27, and Italian boyfriend Lorenzo De Luca, 32, weren't mounted on their horses, they watched the competition from the VIP section and packed on the PDA. 

The Boss and Patti were seated next to the love birds locking lips as they watched the Longines Global Champions Tour Friday. 

Jessica Rae Springsteen, 27, packed on the PDA with Italian boyfriend Lorenzo De Luca while sitting next to her famous father Bruce and mother Patti 

It appears Bruce had to look away and keep his eyes on the competition as his little girl and 32-year-old boyfriend locked lips 

Bruce Springsteen and wife Patti attended the Longines Global Champions Tour in Miami this weekend where his equestrian daughter and fellow pro horse jumper boyfriend competed 

The young couple and rockstar were all smiles as they enjoyed the competition from a large VIP area 

Springsteen wore dark sunglasses and a baseball cap before taking it off and taking a few selfies with fans who approached 

Bruce and Patti attended all three days of the competition over the weekend, sitting at the large VIP section and blending in with the crowd.

The four appeared to enjoy the games and were all seen leaving together, with Jessica and Lorenzo holding hands in their first time pictured being affectionate in public. 

Bruce remained low-key, wearing a white T-shirt with a dark jacket, a cap and sunglasses. 

Sources tell DailyMail.com that the rockstar was approached only a couple of times for selfies with fans, and appeared happy to take them.   

On Saturday, Jessica showed off her jumping skills atop her $4million horse.

Jessica, the second of three children of the rocker and his longtime wife, was seen wearing a grey and white shirt and white pants before the race.

During the race she covered her shirt with a navy blue coat, with black boots and a black riding helmet.

Jessica was a reserve rider for the U.S. Equestrian team at the 2012 London Olympics but she fell short of making the team at the 2016 Rio Olympics.

She started riding when she was just five years old, after she moved to a New Jersey horse farm with her parents.

After moving to show jumping, she earned medals at the 2010 North American Young Rider Championships, and was part of Senior Nations Cup teams.   

The Longines Global Champions Tour is an annual show jumping series that comprises up to 15 rounds of competition hosted around the world.
