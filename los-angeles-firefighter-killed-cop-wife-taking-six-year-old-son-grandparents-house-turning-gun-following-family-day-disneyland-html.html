LA firefighter killed cop wife Cecilia Hoschet and hiimself after a day at Disneyland

 Posted on August 25, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  694 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Murdered: County deputy Cecilia Hoschet was killed by her husband firefighter James Taylor

A Los Angeles firefighter shot dead his policewoman wife before turning the gun on himself after a family day out at Disneyland on Sunday.

James Taylor killed partner Cecilia Hoschet, 32, while their six-year-old son was in the house before taking the boy to his grandparents' home.

After dropping the child off, Taylor, who worked as a paramedic for six years, drove to a fire department warehouse where he worked about 15 miles away and shot himself. 

The tragic incident unfolded at the couple's house in the Los Angeles suburb of La Canada, Flintridge, on Sunday night.

Ms Hoschet worked at the county jail's inmate reception center after joining the Sheriff's Department two years ago.

Sheriff's deputies responded to a 911 call just before 10pm that a woman was screaming inside the home and they arrived shortly after to find the body of Ms Hoschet.

Taylor was found dead at his fire department with a single gunshot wound and had his radio with him - with some saying he had used it to alert authorities he had killed his wife.

'Today is a sad day for all public safety personnel in Los Angeles County,' county fire Chief Daryl L. Osby said in a written statement.

The sheriff's department said it recovered a weapon but was not publicly discussing a possible motive.

A parking receipt found in the couple's car suggested the family had been to Disneyland that day as it was dated Sunday September 6.

The couple had financial difficulties in the past and filed for bankruptcy in 2010 after an investment property they had purchased in Canyon Country became caught up in debt.

But they had emerged from bankruptcy two years ago, reports the LA Times.

The motive for the horrendous crime is still uncertain and the couple had no known history of domestic violence, no restraining orders and no divorce proceedings.

Sheriff’s Lt. David Coleman said: 'We have no indication of why this tragedy occurred. He didn’t say anything to explain.'

Both Ms Hoschet and Taylor were paramedics. Before joining the Sheriff’s Department two years ago, Ms Hoschet worked as a paramedic for a private ambulance firm and for the city of Sierra Madre. 

The Los Angeles Times reported yesterday that detectives were investigating the possibility that Taylor used his work radio to tell authorities about his wife's slaying — and that he was going to kill himself.

According to a scanner recording reviewed by the newspaper, soon after the 911 call, a man got on an emergency services channel and said responders would find one person dead at the home.

Sheriff's Deputy Amber Smith would not comment on the report about the radio call, saying the case was still under investigation.
