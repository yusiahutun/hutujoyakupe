HISTORICAL EVENTS

  • 1922: Egypt gains independence from Britain.
  • 1945: The United Nations is officially founded.
  • 1961: The Soviet Union detonates the Tsar Bomba, the most powerful nuclear weapon ever detonated.
  • 1983: The first African-American woman, Guion Bluford, goes to space aboard the Space Shuttle Challenger.
  • 2005: The trial of Saddam Hussein, the former President of Iraq, begins.

SCIENTIFIC BREAKTHROUGHS

  • 1948: The Bell Labs announces the invention of the transistor, a milestone in the field of electronics.
  • 1974: The first successful commercial microprocessor, the Intel 8080, is introduced.
  • 1991: The World Wide Web becomes publicly available, revolutionizing information sharing on the internet.
  • 2001: The Mars Odyssey spacecraft begins its mission to study Mars’ climate and geology.
  • 2018: The world’s first gene-edited babies are born in China, raising ethical concerns.

POLITICAL MILESTONES

  • 1485: Henry VII becomes the King of England after his victory at the Battle of Bosworth Field, marking the end of the Wars of the Roses.
  • 1864: The Nevada Territory becomes the 36th state of the United States.
  • 1918: The Ottoman Empire signs the Armistice of Mudros, ending its participation in World War I.
  • 1953: British Prime Minister Winston Churchill announces that the United Kingdom has successfully developed a hydrogen bomb.
  • 1998: The Good Friday Agreement is signed, bringing an end to the Troubles in Northern Ireland.

  • 1981: Ivanka Trump, businesswoman and daughter of Donald Trump, is born in New York City.
  • 1993: Theo James, English actor known for his role in the “Divergent” series, is born in Oxford, England.
