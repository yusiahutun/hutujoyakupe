Gemma quickly made a name for herself in the modelling world after making her Australian Fashion Week debut at the tender age of fifteen.

It wasn't long before her unique baby doll features scored her multiple New York runway gigs with the Aussie stunner rumoured to be earning $20,000 per show at the height of her career.

Back in 2004 at the age of sixteen, she became the youngest model to appear in American Vogue, cementing her status as one of the hottest models of the moment.

Her career went from strength to strength, with the Perth-born beauty securing roles in Hollywood films such as The Strangers, Pirates of the Caribbean: On Stranger Tides and The Great Gatsby.

Despite her success, Gemma began to shy away from the spotlight, keeping a low-profile since 2008, following the death of her then-boyfriend Heath Ledger. 

At the time of Ledger's death, she said: 'there was no one like him and no one will ever be like him.' 

Moving on from her past romance, in December 2013 the cover girl and her beau David welcomed their daughter Naia into the world.

Having a rest: Young Naia rubbed her eyes, suggesting she was tired from the eventful weekend

The starlet is notoriously private and managed to keep her pregnancy quiet for six months before it was revealed in August last year. 

But after a six-year hiatus which involved tackling motherhood, Gemma has finally made her long-awaited comeback.

The clothes horse is now the face of Australian fashion and lifestyle brand, Country Road which is celebrating its 40th anniversary this year.
