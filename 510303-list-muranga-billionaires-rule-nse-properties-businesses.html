List of Muranga Billionaires Who Rule NSE, Their Properties and Businesses

  • Another group from Rwathia village in the same county also rose from humble backgrounds to successful businessmen
  • Among Kenyan billionaires from Murang'a are James Mwangi, Benson Wairegi, Peter Munga and James Kibunga Kimani

Murang'a county has produced some of Kenya's richest billionaires who have invested in the Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE).

The Muranga Council of Eminent Persons was launched in 2015 and registered as a company limited by guarantee in 2018.

Members of the council have established multi-billion businesses across the country.

Another group from Rwathia village in the same county also rose from humble backgrounds to successful businessmen.

List of Murang'a billionaires at NSE

1. James Mwangi

James Mwangi is the group managing director of Equity Bank.

The banker who owns a 3.4% stake in the lender earned KSh 685.3 million in dividends after the bank reported a profit of KSh 46.1 billion.

According to Billionaires Africa, however, Mwangi's stake at Equity has decreased by KSh 1.62 billion since the start of 2023.

His stake is now valued at KSh 9.75 billion down from KSh 11.37 billion. The slump was attributed to a sustained decline in Equity Group shares on the NSE.

2. SK Macharia

Samuel Kamau Macharia popularly referred to as SK Macharia is the founder and chairman of Royal Media Services (RMS).

The soft-spoken entrepreneur was born in 1942 in Ndakaini. His parents were squatters in Nakuru county and worked at British plantation farms.

Besides RMS, he has interests in Directline Insurance, Serenity Media Productions Ltd, Big Five Conservancy, Bushfire Media Distributors, Toi Redevelopment and Harbour Capital Ltd.

His total net worth is estimated at over KSh 40 billion.

3. Peter Munga

Billionaire businessman who chairs the Mt Kenya Foundation Peter Munga is another son of Murang'a county.

He founded Equity Bank (previously Equity Building Society) in 1984. He retired as the bank's chairman in 2018, aged 75.

According to Forbes, Munga owns a 0.4% stake in the lender worth $5 million (KSh 698.5 million) after selling most of his shares.

Besides Equity, the 79-year-old is a major shareholder in Britam Holdings (over KSh 500 million value) and Equatorial Nut Processors.

4. Benson Wairegi

Former Britam Holdings PLC group managing director Benson Wairegi controls a stake worth over KSh 900 million in the insurance firm according to Who Owns Kenya.

After being at the helm of the company for four decades, he handed over to Zimbabwean Tavaziva Madzinga.

Wairegi first bought shares at the company in 1984 after a new directive by the regulator that required foreign companies to sell at least 33.3% of shares to locals.

To acquire shares, he took a personal mortgage of KSh 600,000 against his house.

5. John Kibunga Kimani

John Kibunga Kimani is the biggest individual Safaricom shareholder with over 16.2 million shares as of 2021.

Kimani's value of his holdings in the NSE is over $25 million (KSh 3.5 billion).

He also owns stakes in different companies such as Kakuzi, Nationa Media Group, and Total Energies where he sold three million shares valued at $800,000 (KSh 111.7 million) in 2019.

Joe Kibe's death

Joe Kibe, the former Council of Eminent Persons of Murang’a chair died on December 1, 2021, while undergoing treatment at the Aga Khan Hospital.

The businessman was a one-time permanent secretary in the Public Service ministry in Mzee Jomo Kenyatta’s administration.

Kibe also had a stint at the Kenya Horticulture Crops Development Authority in which he served as the chair. Besides his roles in government, the deceased was a known investor in the various sectors of the economy.

Simon Gicharu's inspiring story

In other news, Mt Kenya University founder Simon Gicharu rose from a milk vendor to a billionaire.

He was born in 1964 in Gathiruini village, Kiambu county, to peasant parents, and he struggled in his early life.

His entrepreneurial journey started after he was fired from his job as a teacher by the Teachers Service Commission (TSC).

After his sacking, he started selling milk to support his family, considering his parents were peasants.

Besides MKU, he is behind Cape Media Ltd which owns TV and Radio 47.
