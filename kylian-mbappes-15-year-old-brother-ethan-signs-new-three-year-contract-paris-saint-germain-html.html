Kylian Mbappe's 15-year-old brother Ethan signs new three-year contract with Paris Saint-Germain

 Posted on September 14, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  322 words  |  Martina Birk

Kylian Mbappe's brother Ethan has signed a new three-year contract with Paris Saint-Germain.

15-year-old Ethan Mbappe has put pen to paper on a new deal that will run to the summer of 2024, as reported by the club's official website. 

The younger Mbappe is capable of playing up front or out wide and impressed at the International Champions Cup Futures tournament in 2019.

Ethan Mbappe wrote on Instagram: 'I am very proud to continue with my team, PSG'.


PSG also announced the signing of youngster Senny Mayulu, who was born in 2006.

While Ethan Mbappe's future has been secured, there remains much uncertainty over whether Kylian will remain at the club.

Ethan Mbappe, 15, has impressed for PSG at youth level and can play up front or out wide

There remains uncertainty over whether Kylian Mbappe (L) will remain at PSG this summer

The France star has one year remaining on his current deal and has been heavily linked with a move to Real Madrid.

Since joining PSG, initially on loan, in 2017, Mbappe has scored 132 goals in 171 appearances.

Mbappe has won an array of trophies with PSG, including three league titles, but may elect to leave the club this summer.
