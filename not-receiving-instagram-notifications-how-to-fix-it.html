Not Receiving Instagram Notifications? How to Fix the Problem

 Posted on May 24, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1140 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Have your Instagram app notifications stopped coming through? Users sometimes miss out on their Instagram notifications with changes to device settings or app updates.

If your notifications on Instagram no longer work, try out these fixes.

1. Enable Push Notifications on Your Phone

If push notifications are disabled for Instagram, you won't receive these alerts until you give the necessary permissions to the app.

To check your settings for Instagram on an iPhone, follow these steps.

  • Open your phone's Settings.
  • Find Instagram by scrolling down.
  • Tap Notifications to open the menu.
  • Toggle the Allow Notifications tab to give the app permission.

    Android users can use the following steps:

  • Go to your device Settings and tap Apps.
  • Search Instagram and press Notifications.
  • Turn on the Show notifications option.
    • Close

    Restart the Instagram app by removing it from the background. Your phone will most likely receive notifications now.

    2. Check if You've Paused Notifications on Instagram

    If your phone's push notifications are already on, there is a possibility that alerts are paused from the app itself. Users who do not want to erase their social media presence use the pause notifications feature to reduce screen time.

    If you have paused your Instagram notifications in the past, here's how to undo it:

  • Open your Instagram profile.
  • Tap the three horizontal bars on the top-right.
  • Go to Settings.
  • Select Notifications.
  • Toggle the Pause All tab to enable push notifications.
    • Close

    The steps are the same on an Android device as well. You can also pause specific notifications on Instagram and enable the most important ones. You can also activate Quiet Mode on Instagram for a temporary break.

    3. Restart Your Phone

    Restarting your phone fixes potential glitches by restarting all background processes. Reboot your phone at least once a week to give it a fresh start. Then connect to a stable Wi-Fi network. Once done, you should start receiving Instagram notifications on your device.

    4. Clear the Instagram App Cache

    A corrupted cache might lead to issues with your Instagram app.

    Follow these steps if you use an iOS device:

  • Go to your device Settings and tap General.
  • Scroll down and select Instagram.
  • Tap Offload App to free up some of the storage used by the app.
    • Close

    If you are an Android user, you can clear the cache by following these steps.

  • Go to Settings and navigate to Battery and device care. Then select Storage.
  • Choose Apps and search for Instagram.
  • Scroll down and tap Storage to see how much space Instagram has used.
  • Select Clear Cache to remove all unnecessary files.
    • Close

    5. Check Your Internet Connection

    Close

    Another possibility as to why you are not getting any Instagram notifications is bad internet. Your Wi-Fi connection could be faulty, meaning your app is not syncing new notifications. To ensure that's not the case, check your internet connection.

    Try refreshing the app or loading any video. If it doesn't work, restart your internet device. Restarting usually solves the issue, but if it doesn't, use a troubleshooting guide to fix your internet.

    If your internet problem persists, contact your internet service provider. Switch to Cellular Data if Wi-Fi doesn't seem to respond on your phone.

    6. Check Background Data Usage and Low Data Mode

    Background data usage allows you to manage how much battery and data your phone consumes. Having your phone on low background data might cause a delay in receiving Instagram notifications.

    To check these settings on an Android device:

  • Go to Settings.
  • Tap Connections and select Data Usage.
  • From there, go to Wi-Fi Data Usage.
  • Find Instagram and toggle the Allow background data usage to turn it on.
    • Close

    Since Android models come with a bit of variation in the interface, you might find the same settings differently. Once the restrictions on your data usage are lifted, you should start receiving alerts on Instagram.

    iPhone users have an option of Low Data mode to conserve their cellular data. If you are not receiving Instagram notifications on your iOS device, make sure the Low Data mode is turned off. Here's how to check it:

  • Go to Settings and select Cellular.
  • Press Cellular Data Options.
  • Check Low Data Mode. It should be turned off.

    7. Update Your Instagram App

    Close

    An outdated app can also be a reason why your Instagram is acting up. Go to the Play Store or App Store to check if an update is due. You will see the option to update if a newer version is available.

    An update doesn't necessarily mean a dramatic change in your app's interface. Most of them just fix bugs or get rid of errors. Maybe a bug is preventing you from receiving Instagram notifications. Itâ€™s better to stay updated.

    8. Update Your Phone

    A new mobile update comes with several features and fixes that you might need for apps to function properly.

    The best practice is to update your phone as soon as you receive an update alert. For instance, a notification-based error in older versions can be fixed after your phone is updated.

    To update your iPhone, go to Settings > General > Software Update. Download and install the update if it's pending.

    Close

    For Android, go to Settings > Software update > Download and install and check if an update is available.

    Close

    People with outdated phones should consider upgrading, as older phones also retain security-related issues. Certain warning signs indicate it's time to change your phone to a newer model.

    9. Turn Off Airplane Mode and Do Not Disturb Mode

    Close

    Airplane Mode is a setting that blocks your phone's ability to receive messages, make calls, and connect to the internet. People often use it to save their phone's battery or to temporarily stop receiving text messages.

    You might have put your phone on Airplane Mode and now aren't receiving any alerts. Turn the Airplane Mode off from your phone for it to function properly.

    10. Reinstall Instagram

    If every other method fails, you can uninstall and reinstall Instagram. This not only gives you the most recent version but also eliminates any errors. Reinstalling the app clears its cache automatically, giving you a fresh start. Reinstalling the app should solve the issue, but you must keep up with the latest Android or iOS updates for maximum benefits.

    Keep Your Instagram Updated at All Times

    Instagram is a highly engaging app, but sometimes users stop receiving notifications. It can be quite frustrating when that happens. You can try out different steps, like checking your internet connection and enabling notifications on the app and phone.

    If nothing seems to work, restart the device or reinstall Instagram to remove all glitches. Your phone should be updated to the latest operating system for better security and to eliminate any glitches that might prevent you from receiving notifications on your phone.
