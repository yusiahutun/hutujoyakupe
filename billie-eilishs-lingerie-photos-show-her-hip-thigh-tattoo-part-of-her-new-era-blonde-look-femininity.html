In her new 'pin-up inspired' look for British Vogue on Sunday, May 2, Billie Eilish gave fans glimpses of the ink she got in 2020. At the time, she said in an interview, “I did get a tattoo. But you won't ever see it.” However, it seems the multiple Grammy award-winning singer had a change of heart about her earlier decision because, in her latest photoshoot, she has partially shown her massive tattoo. 

The 'Bad Guy' singer, famous for choosing to wear baggy clothing in public, wore figure-hugging ensembles for the shoot -- from the custom Alexander McQueen dress with Atsuko Kudo latex stockings and gloves to a Mugler catsuit and corset. The semi-sheer Burberry corset she sported in one photo gave fans a glimpse of her tattoo, which runs from her hip to her thigh.

Eilish posted pictures from the sexy photoshoot for the cover of British Vogue magazine's June 2021 issue on her Instagram page. Though it was not easy to get a clear idea of what the tattoo looked like, her eagle-eyed fans on social media claimed it's a slithering dragon design.

The 19-year-old singer reportedly decided to flaunt her tattoo as part of her new style era, as she recently dyed her hair blonde. She told Vogue, she was “ready for it to suck”, but on contrary, she felt “more like a woman, somehow.”

Gucci creative director Alessandro Michele, who has regularly collaborated with Eilish, said to Vogue, “Billie represents something completely new. She is a continuously evolving artist with a new vision and interpretation of herself in terms of femininity. Billie is exactly where she means to be without prejudice. That’s why working with her is so stimulating – she forces me to think differently.”

Elsewhere in the interview, Eilish made some powerful comments, like, “Suddenly you’re a hypocrite if you want to show your skin, and you’re easy and you’re a slut and you’re a whore. If I am, then I’m proud. Me and all the girls are hoes, and f**k it, y’know? Let’s turn it around and be empowered in that. Showing your body and showing your skin – or not – should not take any respect away from you.”

She also added, “I really think the bottom line is, men are very weak. I think it’s just so easy for them to lose it. ‘You expect a dude not to grab you if you’re wearing that dress?’ Seriously, you’re that weak? Come on! Go masturbate!”
