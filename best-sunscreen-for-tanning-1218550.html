    What to Consider When Buying Sunscreen for Tanning?

    Choosing the right sunscreen for tanning can be tricky. Our list can certainly help you reduce the noise and create the right pool of products. However, you will need to put some time and effort into making the right pick. Here are some key factors to consider when buying the best tanning sunscreen.

    Sun Protection Factor (SPF)

    The first and most crucial factor to consider is the SPF level. A low SPF will certainly help you with tanning, but it might not be good for the skin. Therefore, you must aim to achieve the right balance between these two factors. Most people often wonder what is the best SPF for tanning. You can choose a sunscreen with a low SPF (between 10-15) as it will allow you to tan but still provide some protection against harmful UV rays.

    Skin Type

    Another important factor to consider is your skin type. For dry skin, a sunscreen tanning lotion with added hydrating, skin-loving ingredients, such as shea butter, aloe vera, and vitamin E would work wonders. At the same time, oily skin would best benefit from a sunscreen containing non-comedogenic ingredients and a lightweight and oil-free formula. If you have sensitive skin or burn easily, opt for a higher SPF and reapply frequently. A good tanning sunscreen must be skin-friendly.

    Water Resistance

    Consider choosing a sunscreen that is resistant to water if you plan to spend a long time in the water, such as swimming in a pool or ocean. This will provide continuous protection for your skin even when it's wet, keeping you safe from the harmful effects of the sun.

    Texture

    Sunscreens are available in different textures â€” lightweight, gel-like, or spray-on. Choose any based on your requirements and skin type.

    Choice of Ingredients

    Always avoid something you are allergic to when it comes to buying skincare products. Choose a sunscreen that makes you tan but is free of harmful ingredients such as PABA, parabens, and oxybenzone. You can opt for sunscreens that contain antioxidants and moisturizing oils to nourish your skin while you tan.

    Pro Tip

    Apart from the basic ingredients, look for hydrating and nourishing ingredients like aloe vera, cucumber, coconut, and honey to keep your skin barrier safe and healthy.

    FAQ's

    Is SPF 30 or 50 better for tanning?

    A low SPF count is usually better if your goal is to achieve tanning.

    Do you tan faster with lower SPF?

    Yes, a lower SPF count can help you tan faster. However, it might damage your skin because it offers less protection against harmful sun rays.

    Do you tan slower with higher SPF?

    Yes, using a higher SPF sunscreen can slow down the tanning process. The reason for this is that SPF measures the amount of UVB rays that a sunscreen can block.

    How do you tan and not burn?

    Tanning without burning requires a careful balance of sun exposure and protection.

    Do sunscreens prevent tanning?

    No, sunscreens donâ€™t prevent tanning. They just shield your skin from 100% of UVA radiation.

    Does sunscreen make your skin darker?

    No, but if it darkens your skin, you should consult a dermatologist to check if the sunscreen contains any darkening agents.

    Do you tan quicker without sunscreen?

    Yes, you tan quicker without sunscreen.

    How long does it take to tan with sunscreen?

    It takes around 1-2 hours to tan with sunscreen.

    Does coconut oil help you tan?

    Why do some people tan more than others?

    Are there sunscreens specifically designed for face and body tanning?

    Yes, there are sunscreens specifically designed for face and body tanning. The facial sunscreens have a milder formula than the ones for the rest of the body.

    Can I use sunscreen with bronzer or self-tanner for enhanced tanning results?

    It is usually best to not use sunscreen with a bronzer or self-tanner, as it may change the measured SPF.
