Jennifer Aniston Recently Opened up About Going to Therapy

 Posted on January 1, 0001  |  2 minutes  |  235 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Jennifer Aniston is opening up about how she stays so positive, even during the tough times. During an interview for People, the Friends star revealed where she's mentally at these days.

"I'm in a really peaceful place. I have a job that I love, I have people in my life who are everything to me, and I have beautiful dogs [schnauzer mix Clyde, pit-bull mix Sophie and newly rescued Great Pyrenees mix Lord Chesterfield]," she said. "I'm just a very fortunate and blessed human being."

The Morning Show actress went on to share how she's gotten to the peaceful place she's in. And spoiler: she's just human like the rest of us.

"Self-awareness is key," she told publication. "I've really gotten a lot out of therapy. Just being a public person, there's a lot of amazing things that come with that. But there's also a lot of tough stuff, because we're only human, and we tend to walk around with bulls-eyes on our heads."

Another key part to Aniston's positive mindset? Watching the sunset every day.

"It's so simple," she explained. "There's no real markers for it, but there are just moments where you just feel ... ahhhh." At the end of the day (both literally and figuratively), Aniston just hopes to have made people laugh.

"I have a good heart, and I'm a great friend," she said. "I lead with love."
