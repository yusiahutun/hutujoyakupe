List of Richest Kenyan Footballers, Their Estimated Net Worth and Properties

  • Among them are Harambee Stars captain Michael Olunga, Victor Wanyama, Arnold Origi and Jamal Mohammed

Even though Kenya is renowned for its athletic prowess, it has produced talented footballers who have made the country proud on the international stage in the last two decades.

Kenya's top footballers who ply their trade abroad take home millions of shillings in salaries and bonuses.

They have accumulated massive wealth and live a flashy lifestyle. Among them are Victor Wanyama, his brother McDonald Mariga, Michael Olunga and Jamal Mohammed.

1. Victor Wanyama's wealth

Professional footballer Victor Mugubi Wanyama is a household name in Kenya.

The defensive midfielder plays for the United States Major League Soccer (MLS) club CF Montreal.

The 32-year-old began his football career at JMJ Academy. He then joined Kenyan Premier League club Nairobi City Stars and later AFC Leopards before moving abroad.

Among the teams he played for are Celtic, Southampton and Tottenham.

Wanyama joined Montreal in March 2020. He pockets an average of KSh 24 million at the club, bringing his annual income to KSh 290 million. When annual guaranteed compensation is added, his total annual pay rises to KSh 374 million.

Who Owns Kenya estimates Wanyama's net worth at over $15 million (KSh 2.2 billion).

The footballer owns mansions in London and Kilesheshwa Nairobi. He also drives fancy cars including Volkswagen Crafter CR53 Maxi, Range Rover Sport, Mercedes Benz ML350 and Bentley.

2. McDonald Mariga

At the height of his career at Inter Milan, McDonald Mariga earned about KSh 145 million a year.

The footballer-turned-politician showed talent pays as he played for some of the top teams in Europe.

Mariga who lives in a lavish mansion in Nairobi drives luxurious cars befitting his status as a professional retired footballer.

During campaigns in Kibra in 2019, he turned heads after showing up with H2 Hummer which costs upwards of KSh 9 million. His net worth is estimated at $7 million (KSh 984 million).

3. Michael Olunga

Kenyan footballer, Michael Olunga's rise can be described as from grass to grace, having come from a humble background.

The humble talisman had a passion for football while still young and started playing way back in primary school.

The 29-year-old Harambee Stars captain signed for Qatari club Al-Duhail in January 2021 after playing for European and Asian clubs.

His fees stood at €6 million (KSh 921.3 million).

Olunga earns a monthly salary of KSh 6.9 million at the club and his net worth es estimated at $4 million (KSh 562.4 million).

His properties include a Porsche Panamera car worth over KSh 10 million, a Toyota Landcruiser 11 V8 and a mansion in Homa Bay county.

4. Arnold Origi

Origi is ranked among the most successful Kenyan goalkeepers in history.

The 39-year-old began his career at Mathare United before switching to Tusker and then Moss in Norway where his talent shone.

He currently plays for Hareid IL. According to Know Afrika, his wealth is valued at $4 million (KSh 562.4 million).

5. Jamal Mohammed

Jamal Mohammed is regarded as Kenya's former stylish footballer. The 38-year-old plays for the Omani sports club Al-Nasr.

Besides Al-Nasr, he has represented Ghana's Liberty Professionals, Enkonpings of Sweden, Kuwaiti's Kazma and Romanian club Targu Mures.

Janeth Jepkosgei's hustle

Meanwhile, Janeth Jepkosgei was one of the most popular Kenyan athletes during her prime.

The 39-year-old retreated to her home in Nandi after her retirement. She set up the Kapchemoiywo Camp­ to nurture young talents in the county named Source of Champions.

She took the role of a coach and a mentor to over 80 athletes at the camp which started with 40 runners.

Vivian Cheruiyot's wealth

In related news, Vivian Cheruiyot is also one of the most decorated Kenyan athletes, with several medals in her name.

The Pocket Rocket has an estimated net worth of $1.5 million (KSh 210.9 million) accumulated in a stellar career spanning two decades.

The 2018 London Marathon champion has invested in the lucrative real estate sector in Eldoret, one of the fastest-growing towns in Kenya.
