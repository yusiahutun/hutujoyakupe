Emma Stone's La La Land Dress Was Actually Inspired By One of Her Best Red Carpet Looks

 Posted on February 10, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  344 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Part of what makes La La Land so captivating is that it infuses the charm of Old Hollywood into current life in Los Angeles. That notion holds true in the film's musical numbers and technicolor tributes, but it's most obvious in the costumes, from Emma Stone's flowy vintage dresses to Ryan Gosling's skinny tie and wing-tipped shoes. Costume designer Mary Zophres, who already dressed the stars in retro garb for Gangster Squad in 2013, styled Stone and Gosling once again for this film.

Although Zophres noted taking inspiration from musical films from the '60s for La La Land's wardrobe, she had a modern muse when it came to one of Stone's standout onscreen looks—that floral yellow dress (pictured above). Zophres reavealed that she based the frock off the Atelier Versace dress Stone wore to the Amazing Spider-Man 2 premiere in 2014.

Getty Images

"It's stunning on Emma and I pitched [that color] to Damien [Chazelle, the director] for that sequence," she told Fashionista. "The actress fell in love it it after their first fitting," Zophres said. And despite its couture inspiration, Stone's dress itself was only made from polyester from Jo-Ann Fabrics with a "Matisse-inspired" floral pattern painted on by hand.

La La Land and the Spider-Man premiere aren't the only instances where Stone stunned in canary yellow. The redhead has a history of opting for the sunny shade for a red carpet look.

The actress also donned the shade in a Giambattista Valli shift dress with ruffled sleeves at The Hollywood Reporter's Women in Entertainment breakfast this week...

Getty Images

... and on a Rochas dress at the hand and footprint ceremony with Ryan Gosling a few days earlier.

Getty Images
