Metro TV

After graduating from the Kenya Institute of Mass Communication, Dennis Okari got a position as an assistant producer, editor and reporter at Metro TV in 2003. Many remember the famous television station that is no longer on air. It was once one of the fast-rising television stations in Kenya that certainly moulded Dennis' skills as a young journalist.

KBC

During the same period, Dennis worked as a researcher and voice over artist at Kenya Broadcasting Corporation, Kenya’s state-owned television station. The media company is renowned for imparting amazing skills to numerous journalists that are dominating the airwaves today.

TBN Africa

Between 2004 and 2006, Dennis was a presenter and show host for Metro TV and also worked for TBN Africa (Family Media) as a producer and presenter. This laid a good foundation and acted as the infancy developmental milestone towards his flowered career today.

KTN

Dennis’ rise to stardom probably started when he was given a chance to work as a reporter and news anchor at Standard Group Limited. Here, was part of Kenya Television Network’s reporters, multitasking between research and reporting work as well as news anchoring.

He got the Standard Group Limited job in 2009. Shortly after Radio Africa came looking for the talented journalist and appointed him as an anchor and senior investigative reporter. This role continued to polish his investigative skills as a journalist that he has perfected today.

NTV and BBC

Nation Media Group hired Dennis Okari as a senior investigative crime reporter in 2013, a role that perfectly fits his style. On top of this, Dennis is a multi-platform bilingual reporter on BBC News covering security, governance, and feature stories. He got this position in 2014 and has been working here for six years.

He is famous for covering war-torn areas, crime scenes, investigating the profound and sensitive matters and exposing some unethical practices in society. Numerous platforms have recognized Dennis Okari’s investigative work, and in 2014, he was named the Journalist of the Year by the Media Council of Kenya.
