Richard Bunt dies after falling from a shuttle in Krakow

 Posted on April 1, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  527 words  |  Aldo Pusey

A tourist has died in a freak accident after he fell out of an electric shuttle while trying to retrieve his sunglasses.

Richard Bunt, a 33-year-old company director who lived in London, was on holiday with close friends and family in Krakow, Poland, when he fell out of the vehicle and cracked his head.

He suffered a severe bleed on the brain and passed away in the city's hospital five days later, leaving his wife and family distraught.

Richard Bunt, a 33-year-old company director pictured with his wife Lucy, 32, suffered a fatal bleed on the brain after falling from a shuttle in Poland

Richard's wife Lucy, 32, younger brother Andrew, 29, and parents Ann and Nigel were at his bedside when he died.

His organs were donated to help save the lives of six others.

Richard was travelling to his accommodation in an electric tourist vehicle on June 9 when he dropped his sunglasses.

As he leaned out to try and pick them up, he fell out and hit his head.

Richard, second from left, was travelling to his accommodation in an electric tourist vehicle on June 9 when he fell, and died in hospital five days later

He received emergency treatment at the scene before being rushed to Krakow University Hospital.

Richard had emergency surgery to stem the bleed on his brain and was placed into a coma as medics battled to save his life.

However, on Tuesday, doctors at the hospital, along with specialists in the UK, pronounced him clinically dead.

In his final hours, Richard donated his organs to Polish patients in need of treatment, with the hospital confirming he helped six people.

Richard, originally from Kenilworth, Warks, worked as the sales and marketing director at Timex Group UK, having previously worked for retail giant Amazon.

He was a keen footballer and golfer, and only two months ago watched Coventry City beat Oxford United in the Checkatrade Trophy final at Wembley. 

He was on holiday with close friends and family in Krakow, Poland (pictured), when the tragic incident occurred 

In a statement, his parents said: 'It is with deep sadness that we announce the loss of our wonderful son, Richard, who passed away on June 13 after a tragic accident in Krakow.

'He brought so much happiness to our lives and was a loving husband, caring son and brother, friend and colleague.

'We take great strength from knowing that his final gift was his greatest of all - donating his organs to save six people - giving so much joy and hope to other families.

'He will always be treasured by us and by everyone who knew him and we are immensely proud of the man he was.

'We would like to thank all the kind people who helped and prayed for Richard and also thank our friends and family around the world.'
