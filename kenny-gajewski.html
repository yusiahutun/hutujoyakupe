Kenny Gajewski is an American softball coach. Under Coach Kenny Gajewski’s leadership, Oklahoma State softball has experienced a remarkable transformation. With three consecutive Women’s College World Series appearances, a conference title in 2022, and consistent NCAA postseason play, the Cowgirls have reestablished themselves as a national power.

Kenny’s player-centered philosophy is evident in his decision-making, and he prioritizes the team’s needs. The annual team retreat in Broken Bow, Oklahoma, has become a tradition that fosters camaraderie and has yielded positive results.

Gajewski’s ability to attract high-level talent and create a winning culture is evident, with multiple players earning All-America honors. Overall, Gajewski’s dedication and player-focused approach have propelled Oklahoma State softball to new heights.

Early life

Kenny Gajewski was born on October 23, 1971, in Los Alamitos, California. He attended Los Alamitos high school. After high school, he went to Cerritos College. He has spent a year as a red shirt there. He played under Falcon coach Dave Serrano. For the remaining two years of his collegiate sports career, he transferred to Oklahoma. He graduated from the University of Oklahoma.

Kenny Gajewski Career

Kenny played baseball for one season at Cerritos College then transferred to Cal State Dominguez Hills for the next two years. In 1994 he finally transferred to Oklahoma University and joined the Sooners. The University won the National championship that year. The win helped him bag the post of graduate assistant coach at Oklahoma University in the following season.

Oklahoma State Softball 

Kenny worked there for three years before joining the Oklahoma State softball program as the head coach. Since then Oklahoma State softball team has participated in three consecutive Women’s College World Series. In 2022, the team earned its first conference title of the Big 12 era. The amazing performance helped the team establish itself as one of the best teams at the national level.

After his arrival, the Cowgirls have made it to the NCAA postseason every year. Kenny has brought several significant changes to the team. It is considered one of the greatest program transformations.

Kenny organizes a team trip every year to Broken Bow, Oklahoma, which helps the new team players become familiar with each other. The trip has a lot of positive effects on the team’s performance. Kenny motivates his players to focus on their academics too.

The increased number of honors and academic rewards depicts the coach’s efforts. Kenny owns three Athletics turf in Norman, Oklahoma- Champions Athletic Turf, Ameriturf MidSouth, and Sooner Curb and Border. Still, his complete focus is on his softball coaching career.
