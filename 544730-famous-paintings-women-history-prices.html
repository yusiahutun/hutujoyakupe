1. Les Demoiselles d’Avignon

  • Artist: Pablo Picasso
  • Year: 1907
  • Medium: Oil on canvas
  • Price: $1.2 billion

Les Demoiselles d'Avignon is a revolutionary painting portraying five naked women in a brothel. At the time, his painting was a departure from the traditional single viewpoint prevalent then. Picasso presented viewers with multiple perspectives, which is evident in the poses assumed by the women.

2. Mona Lisa

  • Artist: Leonardo da Vinci
  • Year: 1503–1506
  • Medium: Oil on poplar panel
  • Price: $1.029 billion

The Mona Lisa, a Renaissance famous painting of a woman, continues to captivate audiences worldwide. The half-length 16th-century painting features a woman with an enigmatic smile against a distant landscape. The Mona Lisa is one of the world's most visited, best known, most written about, most sung about and most parodied works of art.

3. Lady with an Ermine

  • Artist: Leonardo da Vinci
  • Year: 1489–1491
  • Medium: Oil on walnut panel
  • Price: $450 million

The Lady with an Ermine is one of the four surviving portraits of women painted by Leonardo da Vinci. It features a woman, Cecilia Gallerani, a mistress of Ludovico Sforza, the Duke of Milan, holding an ermine.

4. Ginevra de’ Benci

  • Artist: Leonardo da Vinci
  • Year: 1474–1478
  • Medium: Oil paint
  • Price: $450 million

Ginevra de' Benci is among the most famous paintings of women. It features a portrait of the 15th-century Florentine aristocrat Ginevra de' Benci, who scholars believe was celebrating her engagement.

The painting is at the National Gallery of Art in Washington, DC, US. According to the Guinness Book of World Records, The art gallery acquired it from Franz Joseph II, Prince of Liechtenstein, in February 1967 for a then-record price of between $5 and $6 million.

5. The Kiss

  • Artist: Gustav Klimts
  • Year: 1907–1908
  • Medium: Oil and gold leaf on canvas
  • Price: $195 million

The Kiss is an oil on canvas painting depicting a couple embracing each other with their bodies entwined in elaborate, beautiful robes. The painting currently hangs in the Österreichische Galerie Belvedere museum in the Belvedere, Vienna.

6. Portrait of Adele Bloch-Bauer I

  • Artist: Gustav Klimt
  • Year: 1903–1907
  • Medium: Oil and gold leaf on canvas
  • Price: $135 million

Portrait of Adele Bloch-Bauer I, also known as The Lady in Gold or The Woman in Gold, is an oil and gold leaf painting. It depicts Adele Bloch-Bauer, a prominent Viennese figure, in an ornate gown against a golden background.

7. Woman with a Parasol – Madame Monet and Her Son

  • Artist: Claude Monets
  • Year: 1875
  • Medium: Oil on canvas
  • Price: $110.7 million

Woman with a Parasol – Madame Monet and Her Son (The Stroll) is an Impressionist work from the 1870s. It depicts Claude's wife, Camille Monet, and their seven-year-old son, Jean Monet, strolling on a windy summer's day.

8. Lady with Fan

  • Artist: Gustav Klimt
  • Year: 1917–1918
  • Medium: Oil on canvas
  • Price: $108.4 million

Lady with a Fan was the final portrait created by Austrian painter Gustav Klimt, painted in 1917. It is a non-commissioned piece depicting an unidentified woman, possibly a dancer, adorned in a patterned silk robe and holding a fan. The background features oriental motifs.

9. Portrait of Madame X

  • Artist: John Singer Sargent
  • Year: 1883–1884
  • Medium: Oil on canvas
  • Price: $21 million

Portrait of Madame X, one of the world's famous paintings of women, features a young socialite, Avegno Gautreau, dressed in a black satin dress. However, the painting received a controversial reception at the Paris Salon of 1884.

10. The Birth of Venus

  • Artist: Sandro Botticelli
  • Year: 1485
  • Medium: Oil on canvas
  • Price: $92.2 million

The Birth of Venus is one of Sandro Botticelli's famous works and one of the most beautiful paintings of women. It depicts the goddess Venus emerging from the sea as a grown woman heading towards the shore with the Horai awaiting to dress her. The portrait depicts a scene in art referred to as Venus Anadyomene.

11. Portrait of Dora Maar

  • Artist: Pablo Picasso
  • Year: 1937
  • Medium: Oil on canvas
  • Price: $67.4 million

Portrait of Dora Maar is among the world's most famous paintings of women's faces. The artwork features Dora Maar, who was Picasso's lover, in elegant attire with a relaxed posture. Picasso painted Maar in the Surrealist style, using bright colours and angular shapes.

12. Olympia (Manet)

  • Year: 1863–1865
  • Medium: Oil on canvas
  • Price: $65 million

Olympia, one of history's famous paintings of women, depicts a naked white woman, Olympia, lying on a bed and attended by a black maid. The painting is renowned for its bold nature, which received a controversial reception at its release.

13. A Bar at the Folies Bergère

  • Artist: Édouard Manet
  • Year: 1882
  • Medium: Oil on canvas
  • Price: $38 million

A Bar at the Folies Bergère is a significant painting by Edouard Manet that historians consider his last major work. It features a barmaid at the renowned Folies Bergère nightclub in Paris. The central figure is a blonde barmaid standing behind a marble counter with assorted bottles on the sides.

14. Ophelia

  • Artist: John William Waterhouse
  • Year: 1851–1852
  • Medium: Oil on canvas
  • Price: $37 million

Ophelia is a captivating artwork depicting Ophelia, a character from Shakespeare's Hamlet, sitting on a willow branch over a pond of lilies in the last moments before her death. Her royal dress contrasts with her natural surroundings.

15. Whistler's Mother

  • Artist: James McNeill Whistler
  • Year: 1871
  • Medium: Oil on canvas
  • Price: $36 million

Whistler's Mother, also known as Arrangement in Grey and Black No. 1, is an iconic painting by American-born artist James Whistler. It features James' mom, Anna McNeill Whistler, dressed in black and seated in a chair.

What is the most famous feminist painting?

According to The Art Story, The Dinner Party (1974-1979) by Judy Chicago is one of the most well-known feminist paintings. It features a large banquet table with place settings for thirty-nine women from history and mythology.
