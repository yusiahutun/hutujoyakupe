Need a last-minute Mothers Day package? Hong Kong hotels offer meals, pampering and childrens

 Posted on June 14, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  611 words  |  Larita Shotwell

It’s just a few days until Mother’s Day and if you haven’t been able to secure a booking for the family at your mum’s favourite restaurant, it might be causing stress and anxiety. No need to panic, we’ve got you covered.

Remove mum from her household duties on a staycation, where meals are taken care of, activities for the little ones are organised, and she can take a breather or make some special memories with her tots.

Hotel Icon offers an in-room, four-course Southeast Asian set dinner, and the package (HK$2,850) includes complimentary breakfast and unlimited access to games such as pool at the Timeless Lounge.

The Four Seasons Hotel Hong Kong’s Mother’s Day staycation package (HK$2,900) includes organised kids’ activities such as a Mother’s Day card design workshop and carnation bouquet workshop.

The hotel has virtual kids’ activities set up with materials provided, where the little ones can take part in online classes. These Mother’s Day virtual workshops include origami heart making, a scavenger hunt, or learning to say “Mama I love you” in different languages. Tea for two is also available for in-room dining as well as a complimentary Guerlain’s orchidée impériale beauty gift and hand massage service. Much better than a boring old brunch.

For fashionista mummies, Island Shangri-La’s Bubbles, Blooms & Bliss for Mum staycation package (HK$3,000), includes a bottle of Champagne, a welcome cake, daily breakfast for two, HK$1,000 dining credit and a bespoke scarf styling session by Austrian enamel jewellery brand Freywille including a complimentary silk scarf. There is also a floral jamming workshop.

Mandarin Oriental Hong Kong is offering an ultimate beauty retreat with Valmont room package (HK$2,280), with a range of luxurious beauty gifts from the Swiss cosmetics brand, gift vouchers for a skin consultation, an exclusive shopping privilege, and facial treatment offer at the La Maison Valmont boutique in IFC, as well as a set meal for two adults, either at Cafe Causette or in the room.

You could skip the staycation and go for an all-out spa treatment at W Hotel’s Bliss spa. The “Our Mom-ment Together” package (HK$8,800 for two) includes two Carol Joy treatments: a 90-minute meridian detox body massage experience and 60-minute precious caviar facial.

EstheClinic is offering a two-for-one promotion for face and body treatments, perfect for mother and offspring bonding. Procedures include the OxyPure – Super 3 in 1 Anti-ageing and Hydrating facial treatment (HK$1,800) that enriches the skin with oxygen to reduce the appearance of pores and even skin tone.

For some mum and tot bonding, eco nail bar Urban Chillez is offering discounts for bookings in May. Kids’ mini manicures cost HK$100.

However, if the way to your mama’s heart is through her stomach and a decent brunch is out of reach, don’t forget there are still some walk-in-only restaurants to try your luck.

Pirata Group’s pasta chain Pici has seven locations around Hong Kong. You can treat mum to their special Lasagne per la Mamma (HK$140) available over the Mother’s Day weekend.

Head over to The Pizza Project for Fiori per la Mamma (HK$150), a springtime pizza made especially for Mother’s Day, topped with mozzarella, home-made ricotta and Sicilian anchovy mousse, sliced zucchini and tasty zucchini flowers.

Do not forget about modern izakaya Yardbird and steak frites expert La Vache.

One last tip for those who are reading this at the 11th hour. If all else fails, you could quickly order a Wrap-at-Home Chilli Dumpling Kit (HK$198) from Chilli Fagara for mums who love some spice in their life. After all, who can resist a home-made lunch from their pride and joy?
