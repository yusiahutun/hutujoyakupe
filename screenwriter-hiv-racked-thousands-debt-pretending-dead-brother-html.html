Screenwriter with HIV racked up thousands in debt by pretending to be dead brother

 Posted on April 14, 2024  |  7 minutes  |  1417 words  |  Aldo Pusey

A gay screenwriter has spoken out about how he took on his dead brother's identity to outwit credit card companies and lead a life of excess after an HIV diagnosis.   

Mark Olmsted, who's originally from New York but lives in Los Angeles contracted HIV, in the early 1980s.

Considering the disease to be a death sentence and recovering from the effects of losing his older brother Luke to the virus, Mark pretended to be his sibling for financial gain, but as the years passed the elaborate scheme started to unravel. 

Writer, essayist, and blogger Mark Olmsted (pictured above) who contracted HIV in the 1980's forged his own death certificate and assumed the identity of his dead brother Luke to run up credit card debit and commit insurance fraud as he thought he would eventually die from the disease and would not have to pay back creditors

Mark realized that his HIV was no longer a death sentence and was slowly forced to face the consequences of years of deception and fraud. 

Telling his story to GQ magazine, Mark said he carried on a decade-long con, fueled by a meth addiction to finance a life he kept thinking would end at any moment. 

Mark and Luke Olmsted were born two years apart. Luke was an Eagle Scout who was industrious and athletic.

Mark came out first. Some years later, Luke revealed he was also gay and tried to help his brother navigate the gay scene in New York, where Mark was attending film school.

Mark (pictured above) and Luke Olmsted were born two years apart, both came out as gay, Mark followed by older brother Luke. Both contracted HIV in the 80s

The brothers eventually moved in together and Mark, unlike Luke, had hundreds of partners.

Early in 1982 Mark began to experience what became the symptoms of an HIV infection, including flu-like ailments, and swollen lymph nodes. 

In late 1988, Luke who had since moved to San Diego, came to visit Mark who immediately saw that his brother had lost an alarming amount of weight.

Both brothers confided in each other they had tested positive for HIV. 

Mark said he felt Luke's diagnosis was his fault as he was 'the one who had encouraged Luke to go to bars and pick up strangers.'

Luke, by this stage, an MD, set about trying to find cures for HIV, but to no avail.

Mark (right) and Luke (middle) with other male members of the family in the 1970s

Mark decided to go and live on the West Coast as Luke had done.

He took an administrative position at an ad agency in Los Angeles, writing when he could, while Luke worked at a leading AIDS-medicine clinic.

After Luke's health began to deteriorate and he contracted a type of pneumonia, in January 1991, Luke went to live with his sister Sandra, in Seattle, who took care of him. 

Luke died on February 9, 1991, at the age of 34. 

Mark told GQ how the deceit began, almost immediately after his elder brother's death.

He couldn't cover his rent so he signed checks in Luke's name, draining his brother's bank account of $10,000.

Mark began spending on his dead brother's credit cards after Luke died in 1991 from AIDS

As executor of Luke's will, he was supposed to send copies of his brother's death certificate to agencies and creditors, but he didn't and when Luke's disability payments arrived in the mail, he cashed them in.     

Within a few months of his brother's death, Mark was using his credit cards, probably at the outset when he was 'drunk settling a bar tab,' with the intention of making good on the payments.

The use soon spiraled out of control with Mark partying, traveling, drinking and using Luke's financial means to buy crystal meth, which 'kept alive his libido,' as he considered he was now living on borrowed time.

Thinking he'd found a way out of his mounting debt, Mark crossed the box at the bottom of Luke's credit card bill, insuring the full balance in the event of the cardholder's death.

Mark waited and changed the date of Luke's passing on his death certificate, postdating it to 1992.

Mark and his family in 1974. Mark forged Luke's death certificate changing the date of his death to get the debt he had racked up on his dead brother's credit card successfully written off 

He mailed the forged certificate and when Luke's next credit card arrived there was a balance of zero.      

The fraud didn't stop there though and Mark went on to use all of his brother's credit cards thinking that 'these were his last years and he wanted a good life.' 

Over the next few years, Mark's health went up and down. One of the films that he had written drew interest from Whoopi Goldberg, but the project never got off the ground.

By the mid-90s Mark's credit card debt totaled $30,000. He was using cards in both his name and Luke's.

He proceeded to send off five new forgeries, including a death certificate for himself.

He'd 'validated certified copies' of the death certificate by using a seal with the letters CC, but the small print encircling the 'CC' actually read 'Corona Cougars' - a baseball team. 

Bar one tense moment when a credit card company called to ask why the seal wasn't raised and colored, the forgeries were never discovered. The woman from that particular credit card company didn't pursue the matter.

With his debt reaching $50,000, he went to West Hollywood to apply for Social Security as Luke.  

'This says Luke Olmsted died,' the man at the office said as he consulted his computer.

Mark showed the man his (Luke's) driver's license and the employee acknowledged it must be a mistake.

Another close escape. 

Mark became a meth dealer before he was arrested and eventually served time in prison for possession of a controlled substance for sale and manufacturing false documents

In dire need of more money Mark drove down his T cell count to dangerous low levels so he was paid out $58,000 against a $100,000 insurance policy, he'd taken out.  

He was also using more and more crystal meth. After paying off his credit cards with the $58,000 he began work as a drug dealer. 

His income soon soared.  

After being hospitalized in 2002 Mark religiously stuck with an antiviral-drug regimen and within a year, HIV was undetectable in his blood.

However, living instead of dying, would now have its own problems, Mark told GQ.

His apartment was raided on August 14, 2003 by six narcotics cops in bulletproof vests.

He was arrested and the judge, on being told Mark had HIV, gave him a fine and 300 hours of community service. 

Despite a determination to go straight, Mark's new found resolve didn't last.  

Mark Olmsted and his family in the 70s. After being hospitalized in 2002 Mark religiously stuck with a antiviral-drug regimen and within a year, HIV was undetectable in his blood and he had to face the consequences of his spiraling debts

He wrote to the Los Angeles County Probation Department in a bid to get the community service hours to go away, pretending for one last time to be Luke Olmsted informing them of his brother Mark's death. 

His deception finally caught up with him. On February 5, 2004, 11 officers stormed his apartment and despite Mark claiming to be Luke, officers discovered fake passports and a fake death certificate for Mark.

Mark was indicted on six charges, including possession of a controlled substance for sale and manufacturing false documents.

He was given a 16-month prison sentence at a minimum-security unit at Chino, an Inland Empire prison. Inside he got clean.

Mark now lives in Hollywood, fourteen years since he left prison, where he has a job and some debt on his credit cards that he is paying off monthly, but there won't be any more scams.

Mark said he doesn't think he would have gotten addicted to meth if he hadn't been traumatized by the early deaths of so many men he loved and most of all his brother. 

'He would have paid for rehab. He would have done whatever. I would go to prison for another ten years if I could have him back,' he said.
